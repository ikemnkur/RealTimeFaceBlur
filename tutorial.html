📚 How to Use Face Blur

Complete guide to protecting privacy with AI-powered face blurring

🎥 Webcam Mode Tutorial

Real-time face blurring for live video feeds, perfect for video calls and streaming.

1 Navigate to Webcam Mode

Click on "Webcam" in the navigation menu or visit the webcam page directly.

2 Grant Camera Permission

Click "Start Webcam" and allow browser access to your camera when prompted.

3 Adjust Settings

Configure blur intensity, detection rate, and resolution to suit your needs.

4 Record (Optional)

Click "Start Recording" to capture the blurred output. Download when finished.

💡 Tip: For better performance in video calls, reduce the output resolution to 50% or 25% and increase the detection update rate to 300-400ms.

🎬 Video Upload Tutorial

Process pre-recorded videos with face blurring and download the results.

1 Navigate to Video Mode

Click on "Video" in the navigation menu.

2 Upload Your Video

Click the upload area or drag and drop your video file. Supports MP4, WebM, AVI, and MOV.

3 Preview & Adjust

The video will start playing automatically with blurred faces. Use playback controls to navigate.

4 Record Output

Click "Start Recording Output" to capture the processed video. The recording will follow your video playback.

5 Download

Click "Stop & Download" when finished. Your blurred video will be saved as a WebM file.

⚠️ Note: Recording is limited to 120 seconds (2 minutes) maximum. For longer videos, record in segments.

⚙️ Settings Explained

Setting Description Recommendation
Output Resolution Controls the size of the canvas output. Lower = better performance. 50% for most use cases, 25% for older devices
Blur Radius Intensity of the blur effect. Higher values = more blur. 40-60px for adequate privacy protection
Detection Update Rate How often AI detects faces (in milliseconds). Higher = less CPU usage. 200ms for smooth tracking, 400ms+ for performance
Detection Sensitivity Confidence threshold for face detection. Higher = fewer false positives. 0.75 (default) works well in most scenarios
ℹ️ Performance Tips: If you experience lag or stuttering, try lowering the output resolution to 25%, increasing the detection update rate to 400-500ms, and closing other applications.

🎯 Best Practices

For Real-Time Webcam Use:

  • Use good lighting for better face detection accuracy
  • Position yourself front-facing to the camera
  • Avoid rapid head movements for smoother blur tracking
  • Test settings before joining video calls
  • Close unnecessary browser tabs and applications

For Video Processing:

  • Use original resolution (100%) for final outputs
  • Preview the entire video before recording
  • Adjust blur radius based on video quality and face size
  • For long videos, process in 2-minute segments
  • Ensure faces are visible and well-lit in the source video

Privacy Considerations:

  • All processing happens locally in your browser
  • No video data is uploaded to servers
  • Clear browser cache if working with sensitive content
  • Test with sample videos before using with important content
  • Remember: blur effect may not be 100% perfect in all scenarios

🔧 Troubleshooting

Camera Not Working?

  • Check browser permissions for camera access
  • Ensure no other application is using the camera
  • Try refreshing the page and granting permission again
  • Test with a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge)

Video Won't Upload?

  • Verify the file format is supported (MP4, WebM, AVI, MOV)
  • Check that the video file isn't corrupted
  • Try a smaller video file (under 100MB recommended)
  • Clear browser cache and try again

Performance Issues?

  • Lower the output resolution to 25%
  • Increase detection update rate to 400-500ms
  • Close other browser tabs and applications
  • Ensure adequate device cooling (laptops may throttle when hot)
  • Try using hardware acceleration in browser settings

Faces Not Detected?

  • Ensure adequate lighting on faces
  • Lower the detection sensitivity setting
  • Make sure faces are clearly visible (not obscured)
  • Face the camera more directly
  • Check that the AI model loaded successfully (status indicator)

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is my video data sent to a server?

No. All processing happens locally in your browser using TensorFlow.js. Your video never leaves your device.

Which browsers are supported?

Modern versions of Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari are supported. Chrome generally offers the best performance.

Can I use this commercially?

See our support page for licensing information.

Why is there a 120-second recording limit?

This prevents excessive memory usage in the browser. For longer videos, record in multiple segments.

Can I blur specific people and not others?

Currently, the app blurs all detected faces. Manual selection is not yet supported but may come in future updates.

What AI model is used?

We use Google's BlazeFace model via TensorFlow.js for fast, lightweight face detection.
